RESIDENTS

a new way to connect generations

Residents is a free service that helps connect users to nursing homes around the area. This service acts as the third party between potential visitors and the nursing homes, and can help to ease the process of visiting. With visiting hours, location-based searching, and information about residents, Residents hopes to bring generations together and reduce the risk of nursing home related depression.
The home page shows our mission statement.
Here, users are able to sign up to volunteer at certain homes. They will be able to find homes by location, and see their visiting hours. They will be able to set up a day to volunteer here.
The user will be able to view information on residents at the home they chose, or on any resident at homes in the area. This will show them things like favorite music, favorite game, and favorite snack, to ensure both parties enjoy their time together.

Design Process

Research
To conduct my research, I interviewed five separate people. All of them have had some experience working in a nursing home, and were able to provide insight on the affect of loneliness. I also have my own experience from when I would visit my grandparents in their nursing home. The interviews went as follows.
  1. Molly
    • Me: How long did you work there?
    • Her: Maybe 9 months.
    • Me: During your time there, about how many of the residents had consistent visitors?
    • Her: It was a long time ago, but not a huge number.
    • Me: Was it better around the holidays?
    • Her: There was an uptick around the holidays.
    • Me: Do you think they could have benefitted from spending time with more people?
    • Her: Probably, they would come visit with me at the desk or socialize with each other. It was a combo retirement home/assisted living/nursing facility.
  2. Chrysta
    • Me: How long did you work there?
    • Her: About a year.
    • Me: During your time there, about how many of the residents had consistent visitors?
    • Her: That I know of, out of the 20 or so residents who I was very familiar with only about 6 or 7 had consistent visitors. A lot of the residents don’t come to the dining hall. The dementia floor got even less visitors and they had maybe 30 or so residents.
    • Me: Was it better around the holidays?
    • Her: Holidays to me seemed good, but I mostly saw older people who were still capable of getting things done on their own. Families came more with grandkids or great grandkids. The nursing home always did something special every holiday, and different occasions to keep everyone’s minds occupied and still make them feel loved and special.
    • Me: Do you think they could have benefitted from spending time with more people?
    • Her: Yes definitely. Schools in the area often came with the students who made cards and they would have dessert in the dining room and play games. The residents always had a great time from what I can tell. The nursing home I worked at also did “senior” prom dances and picnics, movie nights, talent shows, and different actives like that where family and friends can come too. Those were always a big hit. You can always tell when someone hasn’t gone a visit in a while though. Their overall mood changes and they’re so sad they won’t come down for meals or finish their meals.
  3. Johanna
    • Me: How long did you work there?
    • Her: I worked there about 10 months.
    • Me: During your time there, about how many of the residents had consistent visitors?
    • Her: Honestly they didn’t have a lot of visitors. A couple would have family come over but it never happened that often. There were 6/7 people in the house.
    • Me: Was it better around the holidays?
    • Her: Some of my residents would go home and spend either a day or a couple days with family. Honestly if they didn’t go home they wouldn’t have anyone visit them.
    • Me: Do you think they could have benefitted from spending time with more people?
    • Her: Oh most definitely. They would always about how they miss their family or how they just want someone to come see them or even want to see them.
  4. Katie
    • Me: How long did you work there?
    • Her: A few months.
    • Me: During your time there, about how many of the residents had consistent visitors?
    • Her: Honestly not many. I would say less than 50 and it was a bigger facility. They would have either be confined to their rooms when it wasn’t a meal time or pushed in wheel chairs and put in a big round area in the hallway. It was incredibly sad and depressing
    • Me: Do you think they could have benefitted from spending time with more people?
    • Her: Oh definitely. Moral would have been so much higher and I their lives would have been prolonged greatly. I think their depression and low attitudes rubbed off onto the staff as well. We had a very high employment turn around rate and I think it was bc of the environment honestly
  5. Andrew
    • Me: How long did you work there?
    • Him: A summer.
    • Me: During your time there, about how many of the residents had consistent visitors?
    • Him: Honestly, I worked in a dining room. Even there, I only saw about 4 families visit. They were a rather wealthy home as well. 4 consistently.
    • Me: Do you think they could have benefitted from spending time with more people?
    • Him: Absolutely. In my opinion, they were more prone to depression when they went long whiles without visitors. They wouldn’t eat, barely leave their rooms, interact with other residents, or even carry on a conversation. When people got visitors, they would be completely different people. They would laugh and smile at everything, tell stories, and really thrive. I think it’s the most important thing to their mental, and consequently sometimes, their physical health.
I created two personas. One volunteer persona and one elder persona.
The first is the volunteer persona. Her name is Hannah, and she works in retail as a grocery store clerk. She is 27 and loves to talk. By working with the public, she establishes connections with many customers. One customer in particular is Bernice, a regular at her store. Bernice tells her that she is moving into a nursing home following the death of her husband, and Hannah does not see her after that. Hannah uses Residents to schedule a time to surprise Bernice in the home, and to make more connections with other residents inside the home.
The second is the senior persona. Her name is Bernice, and her husband recently passed away. Bernice utilizes the Residents app to view nursing homes in her area and see the residents in the homes. She wants to pick a place to live that she is compatible with the others in. Once her information is loaded to the site, she also can suggest edits of her profile by submitting a request to the site admin.
Brainstorming
  1. Insight
    • Theme: loneliness
      1. Need human interaction
      2. Elderly receive less visitors
      3. Boost morale
    • Theme: accessibility
      1. Need an easier way to help others
      2. If it was easier, people may visit more often
      3. Having information accessible can help make the most of each visit
  2. How Might We?
    • How might we help connect generations?
    • How might we help bring company to those who are alone?
    • How might we make visiting easier?
